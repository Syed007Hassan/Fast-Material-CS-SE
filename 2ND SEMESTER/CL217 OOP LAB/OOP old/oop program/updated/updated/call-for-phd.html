Call For PHD Symposium

Goals and Outcomes

The doctoral symposium presents the opportunity to doctoral students to have senior researchers look at their idea, the work thus far accomplished, and offer a different, outsider's opinion on it. ICETST's doctoral symposium brings you experts from a diverse spectrum of specialties within computer science who have volunteered to guide others in their early research careers. Although your short write-up may go through an initial screening, it is not subject to a formal review, allowing you to further develop and publish it. Also, the advice garnered here does not constitute judgement of the quality of your work, with the understanding that the latter is going to grow.


Submission Guidelines
  • Submission Steps:
  • Submissions should be structured around these elements: the motivation and background of the research, the positioning of the research idea among the state of-the-art, the research questions addressed in the study and a description of the proposed research method.
  • For students whose theses have achieved some of the proposed milestones, you may also add the following: the results thus far and their significance to the completion of your research objectives, possible future directions, problems in moving forward, and anything that you regard as a potential recipient of expert advice.
  • Proposal should be submitted in a single PDF file, not exceeding five pages, for review through the link: https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=icetst2020
Registration and Presentation

Students will have to register as authors through the conference website.
